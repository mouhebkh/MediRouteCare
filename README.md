## Problem Statement

The healthcare system regularly faces critical logistical challenges when it comes to time-sensitive and temperature-sensitive products such as blood products and insulin.
Currently, the following problems are particularly prevalent:

+ Delays in the delivery of blood products during emergency situations
+ Lack of transparency and traceability during transport
+ Inadequate communication between hospitals, delivery services, and blood banks
+ Lack of or inconsistent temperature monitoring for temperature-sensitive medications such as insulin
+ Inefficient coordination of logistical processes during medical emergencies

These structural weaknesses lead to delays in care and can significantly jeopardize patient safety in critical situations.

## Solution

The platform connects 
+ hospitals
+ pharmacies
+ blood banks 
+ pharmaceutical companies,
+ medical transport service providers

within a centralized digital system. The goal is to ensure the fast and reliable delivery of time-sensitive medical products. Through the app, medical needs can be recorded directly and transports organized automatically. Available transport service providers are assigned in real time, and deliveries are tracked seamlessly. In addition, the system offers continuous live monitoring of the transport, including temperature control for sensitive products such as insulin. For critical situations, a prioritized emergency mode is available, enabling immediate and preferential processing of particularly urgent deliveries

## What the app is specifically designed to do

1. Coordinate orders
Hospitals or pharmacies can:
* Request blood
* Reorder insulin
* Initiate emergency transport

2. Automatically notify transport companies
The app sends orders to:
* Certified medical courier services
* Refrigerated transport companies

3. Live tracking of the delivery
Similar to package delivery services:
* View the driver’s location
* Estimate delivery time
* Report delays

4. Temperature monitoring (very important!)
Especially for insulin:
* Sensor measures temperature
* Alarm if cooling is incorrect
* Automatic documentation

5. Emergency mode 🚨
For example:
* Blood type is missing at the hospital
* Patient needs insulin immediately
The app automatically searches for:
* the nearest available source
* the fastest transport service

## Market Opportunity

**In Germany:**

The medical logistics market in Germany offers significant growth potential, particularly in the area of temperature-controlled transport, such as blood products, insulin, and biopharmaceuticals. The German healthcare logistics market already reached a volume of approximately $12.5 billion in 2024 and is expected to grow to over $21 billion by 2030, with an annual growth rate of about 9.3%.

The cold-chain logistics sector (temperature-controlled transport of medical products) is also growing strongly: This market was valued at approximately $13.9 billion in 2025 and is projected to rise to around $27.2 billion by 2034.

The key drivers of this growth are:

+ Increasing demand for temperature-controlled medications such as insulin
+ The growing importance of rapid emergency care in hospitals
+ The rising number of chronic diseases (e.g., diabetes)
+ Digitalization of medical supply chains
+ Use of modern technologies such as IoT tracking and real-time monitoring in transportation

Digital platform solutions for connecting hospitals, blood banks, and transportation services are therefore considered particularly future-proof and offer great innovation potential in the German healthcare logistics market.

**In Switzerland:**

The medical logistics market in Switzerland offers significant growth potential, particularly in the area of temperature-controlled transport, such as blood products and insulin. The Swiss healthcare logistics market already had a volume of approximately $2.53 billion in 2024 and is projected to grow to about $4.18 billion by 2032, with an annual growth rate of over 9%.

In addition, the entire healthcare distribution sector in Switzerland has a market volume of approximately CHF 7–8 billion, supported by around 30 specialized companies.

The key drivers of this growth are:

+ increasing demand for temperature-controlled pharmaceutical logistics (cold chain)
+ the increasing number of chronic diseases such as diabetes
+ growing demands for rapid emergency care
+ the ongoing digitalization of medical supply chains
+ high quality and safety standards in the Swiss healthcare system

Digital platform solutions for connecting hospitals, blood banks, and transport services are considered particularly future-proof, as the need for efficient coordination of medical supply chains continues to grow.
